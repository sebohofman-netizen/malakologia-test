Privacy and Cookies Policy

This page contains information about what data is collected and how it is used, in accordance with the applicable personal data protection regulations.

Personal Data Processing Policy of the Society of Polish Malacologists

  1. The controller of personal data is the Society of Polish Malacologists (SPM), a non-governmental scientific organisation based in Poznań, at: 61-614 Poznań, ul. Umultowska 89, e-mail addresses of board members: anna.labecka@uj.edu.pl (President), a.jaszczynska@uj.edu.pl (Treasurer).
  2. This Privacy Policy regulates the protection of personal data and other non-personal data collected from members of the SPM (personal data means information which, by itself or in combination with other data, allows a person to be identified, e.g. name and surname together with a residential address or place of employment, pursuant to Article 4(1) of the General Data Protection Regulation – GDPR). Personal data of SPM members are processed solely in a manner and to the extent compliant with applicable law.
  3. The legal basis for the processing of personal data by the SPM is Article 6(1)(c) and (f) of the GDPR.
  4. The Society of Polish Malacologists may collect the following information:
    1. name and surname of the SPM member;
    2. residential address of a private individual or the name, tax identification number (NIP), and address of the institution employing the SPM member;
    3. e-mail address of the SPM member;
    4. telephone number of the SPM member;
    5. correspondence between SPM members and the authorities of the Society.
  5. Personal data are collected and processed manually for the purpose of fulfilling the statutory duties of the SPM related to:
    1. informing members about scientific conferences and the ongoing activities of the SPM;
    2. informing members about general meetings of the SPM and decisions of the SPM authorities;
    3. collecting membership fees from SPM members.
  6. Personal data are provided by SPM members when joining the Society.
  7. The SPM does not share data with third parties unless additional arrangements have been made with members in this regard.
  8. The collected data are stored in a database within the MS Office package.
  9. Personal data are stored until the member is removed from the list of SPM members.
  10. Personal data are stored in accordance with applicable law, in particular the provisions of personal data protection legislation and the General Data Protection Regulation, taking into account the following principles:
    1. Adequacy principle – only data necessary to achieve a specific processing purpose are processed;
    2. Transparency principle – SPM members are fully informed about what happens to their data;
    3. Accuracy principle – stored personal data are up to date and accurate. SPM members may update their data if inaccuracies are identified;
    4. Integrity and confidentiality principle – necessary measures are applied to ensure the confidentiality and integrity of personal data; safeguards include physical and technological measures restricting access to data and protecting against data loss;
    5. Accountability principle – upon request, full and reliable information may be provided regarding activities carried out on stored personal data.
  11. The controller makes every effort to ensure all physical, technical, and organisational measures necessary to protect personal data against accidental or unlawful destruction, accidental loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure, use, or access, in accordance with all applicable regulations.
  12. In connection with the processing of personal data, SPM members have the following rights:
    1. the right to rectify data – correction of inaccurate, incomplete, or outdated personal data;
    2. the right to partial or complete erasure of data (“right to be forgotten”) – deletion of data processed without justified legal grounds;
    3. the right to restrict data processing solely to storage;
    4. the right of access to data – obtaining information on the purpose and manner of data processing, and access to copies of data;
    5. the right to data portability – indicating another controller to whom data should be transferred, if technically feasible;
    6. the right to object to data processing;
    7. the right to withdraw consent – SPM members may withdraw any previously granted consent at any time.
    Rights may be exercised by sending a message to: anna.labecka@uj.edu.pl, a.jaszczynska@uj.edu.pl
  13. If it is considered that data are processed in breach of the law, a complaint may be lodged with the President of the Personal Data Protection Office.
